General Takeaways

Something to note is the fact that in virtually every district, a higher percentage of economically disadvantaged students score 1s and 2s than those who aren't economically disadvantaged; a higher percentage of the not economically disadvantaged population score 3s and 4s than the economically disadvantaged population

Something else that's interesting however is the fact that in certain districts, economically disadvantaged and not disadvantaged students will still perform similarly. For example, even though a higher percentage of non economically disadvantaged students score a 3 or 4, it's not a much higher percentage than that of the economically disadvantaged students who scored a 3 or 4. So both student populations can perform just as poorly on the test. This tends to happen in districts with a high economically disadvantaged student population (>90%), suggesting that the entire community is underserved in general.
